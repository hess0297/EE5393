# Problem 1: Synthesizing Stochastic Logic

## Background

In stochastic computing, a real number \(x \in [0,1]\) is represented by a random bit stream where each bit is 1 with probability \(x\):

$$
P(X=1)=x
$$

Basic operations:

- **Multiplication (AND gate):**
$$
P(C=1)=P(A=1)P(B=1)=ab
$$

- **Scaled addition (MUX):**
$$
P(C=1)=s\,a+(1-s)b
$$

For general polynomials, we use the **Bernstein polynomial form**.

---

# (a) Implement $f(x)=x-\frac{x^2}{4}$

## Step 1: Convert to Bernstein form (degree 2)

$$
f(x)=b_0|(1-x)^2|+b_1|2x(1-x)|+|b_2x^2|
$$

Expand and match coefficients:

- $b_0=0$
- $b_1=\frac{1}{2}$
- $b_2=\frac{3}{4}$

Thus,

$$
f(x)=0\cdot|(1-x)^2|+\frac{1}{2}\cdot |2x(1-x)|+\frac{3}{4}|x^2|
$$

## Step 2: Circuit

- Two independent stochastic bit streams (probability x) feed a 2-input counter (adder)
- The counter outputs a 2-bit value:
  - 00 → 0 ones  
  - 01 or 10 → 1 one  
  - 11 → 2 ones  
- This count drives the select lines of a 3:1 MUX
- MUX inputs:
  - b0 → selected when count = 0  
  - b1 → selected when count = 1  
  - b2 → selected when count = 2  

$$
[0,\;\tfrac{1}{2},\;\tfrac{3}{4}]
$$

# (b) Approximate $\cos(x)$

## Step 1: Choose polynomial approximation

We approximate $\cos(x)$ on $x \in [0,1]$ using a simple quadratic:

$$
p(x)=1-\frac{x^2}{2}
$$

This is a **second-order Taylor approximation** of $\cos(x)$ about $x=0$, truncated after the $x^2$ term.  
It remains within $[0,1]$ on the interval, making it suitable for stochastic implementation.

---

## Step 2: Convert to Bernstein form

Using the degree-2 Bernstein basis:

$$
p(x)=b_0|(1-x)^2|+b_1|2x(1-x)|+b_2|x^2|
$$

Matching coefficients gives:

- $b_0=1$
- $b_1=1$
- $b_2=\frac{1}{2}$

So,

$$
p(x)=1|(1-x)^2|+1\cdot |2x(1-x)|+\frac{1}{2}|x^2|
$$

---

## Step 3: Circuit

- Use **2 independent stochastic streams** with probability $x$
- Count the number of ones (0,1,2)
- Use a generalized MUX with inputs:

$$
[1,\;1,\;\tfrac{1}{2}]
$$

---

## Final Result

The stochastic circuit implements the approximation

$$
\cos(x)\approx 1-\frac{x^2}{2}
$$

using Bernstein coefficients:

$$
[1,\;1,\;\tfrac{1}{2}]
$$
